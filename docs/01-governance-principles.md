# Governance Principles

This guidance describes the core principles that help organizations establish effective enterprise governance across complex initiatives.

Enterprise governance creates the leadership structures required to align priorities, clarify decision authority, and maintain oversight across multiple programs or strategic efforts. Strong governance does not add unnecessary control. It creates the conditions for faster, clearer, and more accountable decision-making.

## Enterprise Governance vs Program Governance

These principles apply at the **enterprise governance** level, where leadership oversees multiple initiatives or programs across the organization.

Program-level governance operates within an individual initiative and focuses on execution, delivery coordination, and operational reporting.

## Why Enterprise Governance Principles Matter

Organizations often struggle not because leaders are absent, but because governance is unclear.

Common symptoms include:

- competing initiatives with no clear prioritization
- inconsistent decision-making across leadership groups
- unclear escalation paths
- misalignment between strategic goals and active programs
- delayed decisions that affect execution across multiple teams

Governance principles help organizations define how leadership oversight should function before specific structures, committees, or review cadences are put in place.

## Core Governance Principles

### Strategic Alignment

Governance should ensure that major initiatives remain aligned with organizational priorities and business objectives.

### Clear Decision Authority

Leaders should know who has the authority to make which decisions and when escalation is required.

### Appropriate Oversight

Governance should provide sufficient visibility into major initiatives without overwhelming teams with unnecessary review layers.

### Timely Escalation

Risks, conflicts, and resource constraints should be elevated through defined paths before they affect strategic outcomes.

### Transparency and Visibility

Leadership should have access to the information needed to understand initiative health, trade-offs, and decisions in progress.

### Accountability

Governance structures should make ownership visible so that decisions, actions, and follow-through are clear across the organization.


These principles work together to create governance systems that support both leadership oversight and coordinated execution.

## From Principles to Governance Structures

These principles guide the design of practical governance mechanisms used across the organization.

Examples include:
- leadership governance structures
- decision authority models
- escalation paths for major risks or conflicts
- leadership review cadence
- portfolio oversight processes
