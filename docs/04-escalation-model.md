# Escalation Model

This guidance describes how organizations handle issues that cannot be resolved within normal execution structures.

Escalation is a structured process for raising risks, conflicts, or decisions to the appropriate leadership level when they exceed the authority or capacity of the current team or program.

A clear escalation model helps organizations resolve issues quickly while ensuring leadership attention is directed toward the matters that require it most.

---

## Why Escalation Models Matter

In complex organizations, initiatives often involve multiple teams, departments, and leadership groups. When issues arise, teams may struggle to determine where to raise them or who has authority to resolve them.

Without a defined escalation path, organizations often experience:

- unresolved cross-team conflicts  
- delayed decisions affecting delivery timelines  
- repeated discussions without resolution  
- uncertainty about leadership involvement  
- unnecessary escalation of minor issues  

A structured escalation model helps ensure that issues are resolved at the appropriate level while maintaining momentum across programs and initiatives.

---

## Types of Issues That Require Escalation

Most delivery issues should be resolved within program teams. Escalation typically occurs when an issue affects broader organizational priorities or requires leadership authority.

Common escalation scenarios include:

- cross-department resource conflicts  
- strategic prioritization decisions  
- unresolved dependencies between programs  
- major delivery risks affecting business outcomes  
- budget or investment decisions  
- scope changes that affect organizational strategy  

Escalation should occur when the issue **cannot be resolved at the current level of responsibility**.

---

## Escalation Flow

A typical escalation path follows the leadership structure defined in the governance model.

```mermaid
flowchart LR

A[Delivery Teams]
--> B[Program / Initiative Leadership]

B --> C[Governance Committee]

C --> D[Executive Leadership]
```

This structure ensures that issues move through the organization in a controlled and predictable manner.

Most issues should be resolved at the **lowest possible level**, escalating only when broader authority or alignment is required.

---

## Escalation Responsibilities

Each governance level plays a different role in resolving escalated issues.

### Delivery Teams

Teams attempt to resolve operational issues related to execution, dependencies, or implementation.

If the issue affects multiple teams or cannot be resolved within delivery authority, it may be escalated to program leadership.

---

### Program or Initiative Leadership

Program leaders coordinate across teams and attempt to resolve conflicts within the scope of the initiative.

Typical responsibilities include:

- resolving cross-team dependencies  
- adjusting timelines or delivery plans  
- reallocating program resources  

If the issue affects broader organizational priorities or exceeds program authority, it may be escalated to governance structures.

---

### Governance Committees

Governance bodies review escalated issues that require coordination across departments or programs.

Responsibilities may include:

- resolving cross-organizational conflicts  
- prioritizing initiatives or resources  
- approving significant program adjustments  

---

### Executive Leadership

Executive leadership becomes involved when issues affect strategic priorities, major investments, or organizational direction.

Executive escalation typically involves:

- approving major initiative changes  
- resolving strategic conflicts  
- making final prioritization decisions  

---

## Escalation Documentation

Organizations often document escalated issues to ensure visibility and accountability.

Common practices include:

- maintaining escalation logs  
- documenting decisions made during escalation  
- tracking resolution outcomes  
- communicating decisions back to affected teams  

Documenting escalations helps organizations maintain transparency and learn from recurring issues.

---

## Relationship to Program Governance

Escalation models operate across both enterprise governance and program governance structures.

Program-level escalation typically occurs when delivery teams cannot resolve issues within the initiative.

Enterprise governance escalation occurs when issues affect multiple programs or require leadership intervention.
